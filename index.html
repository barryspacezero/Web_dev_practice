Spidey Tribute Page

Spider-man(Peter Parker)


The man who saved a million lives

Here's a timeline of Spider-man's Life:

1962 (Amazing Fantasy #15) -


  • Peter Parker is bitten by a radioactive spider, gaining spider-like abilities.

  • He becomes Spider-Man and initially seeks fame but learns the lesson of "With great power, there must also come—great responsibility" after his Uncle Ben is killed by a thief he let escape.

    • 1963 (The Amazing Spider-Man #1)


  • Struggles to help Aunt May financially, deals with high school bullies, and fights crime.

  • Hired by J. Jonah Jameson at the Daily Bugle as a freelance photographer.

  • Begins fighting various supervillains.

    • Late 1960s


  • Graduates high school and enrolls at Empire State University.

  • Meets Harry Osborn (roommate), Gwen Stacy (girlfriend), and Mary Jane Watson (introduced by Aunt May).

    • 1970 (The Amazing Spider-Man #90)


  • Captain George Stacy, Gwen's father, dies accidentally during a fight between Spider-Man and Doctor Octopus.

    • 1973 (The Amazing Spider-Man #121-122)


  • Gwen Stacy dies after being thrown from a bridge by the Green Goblin.

  • Spider-Man battles and indirectly causes the Green Goblin's death.

    • 1978 (The Amazing Spider-Man #182-183)


  • Proposes to Mary Jane Watson, who initially turns him down.

  • Graduates from college.

    • 1979 (The Amazing Spider-Man #194)


  • Meets and becomes involved with Felicia Hardy, the Black Cat.

    • 1984-1988 (The Amazing Spider-Man #252)


  • Wears a black costume from the Secret Wars miniseries, later revealed to be an alien symbiote.

  • Struggles with the symbiote, which eventually becomes the villain Venom.

    • 1987 (The Amazing Spider-Man #290-292, Annual #21)


  • Proposes to Mary Jane Watson again; she accepts, and they get married.

    • 1988 (The Amazing Spider-Man #310)


  • Returns to graduate studies at Empire State University.

    • 1995-1996 (Clone Saga)


  • Ben Reilly, a clone of Peter, returns and becomes the Scarlet Spider.

  • Tests indicate Ben is the original, Peter is the clone (later revealed to be false).

  • Ben dies saving Peter, confirming Peter is the original.

    • 1998-2001 (The Amazing Spider-Man vol. 2 #13, #28)


  • Aunt May's apparent death revealed as a hoax; she was kidnapped by Norman Osborn.

  • Mary Jane is believed to be dead in a plane crash but is later revealed to be alive; Peter and Mary Jane separate.

    • 2001-2004 (The Amazing Spider-Man vol. 2 #30, #50, #500, #512)


  • Peter becomes a teacher and meets Ezekiel Sims, who suggests a mystical origin for his powers.

  • Aunt May discovers Peter is Spider-Man.

  • Reconciles with Mary Jane.

  • Learns Gwen Stacy had children with Norman Osborn.

    • 2006 (Civil War)


  • Reveals his identity publicly during the Superhuman Registration Act.

    • 2006-2008 (The Amazing Spider-Man #537, #545)


  • Aunt May is critically wounded, leading to a pact with Mephisto to save her life at the cost of Peter and Mary Jane's marriage being erased.

    • 2008-2009 (The Amazing Spider-Man #591, #600)


  • J. Jonah Jameson becomes Mayor of New York City.

  • Jonah's father marries Aunt May.

    • 2010 (The Amazing Spider-Man #638)


  • Norman Osborn creates the Dark Avengers and leads an invasion of Asgard.

  • Peter deals with the fallout of Norman's actions.

    • 2012-2013 (The Amazing Spider-Man #698-700)


  • Doctor Octopus swaps minds with Peter, becoming the "Superior Spider-Man."

  • Peter's consciousness survives in a subconscious form.

    • 2014 (The Amazing Spider-Man #1, #18)


  • Peter reclaims his body and takes charge of Parker Industries.

    • 2015 (The Amazing Spider-Man vol. 3 #4-6)


  • Discovers another person, Cindy Moon, was also bitten by the radioactive spider and becomes Silk.

  • Joins a multiversal spider-army to fight the Inheritors.

    • 2018 (The Amazing Spider-Man vol. 5 #1, #10)


  • Faces isolation from superhero peers due to Mayor Fisk's support.

  • Reconnects romantically with Mary Jane.

    • 2021 (The Amazing Spider-Man #50, #57)


  • Battles Kindred, who possesses other spider-heroes and causes Peter's temporary death.

  • Resurrected by Kindred and continues his adventures.

    • “Do you know what Spider-Man is? He’s a hand grenade with a bad haircut! Every time you hear about him, there’s a car wreck, a fire, or something destroyed! And when he’s not causing mayhem, he’s nowhere to be found, hiding in the shadows! The real heroes are out there every day, putting their lives on the line in the open, not skulking around like rats!”

    - J.Jonah Jameson
